## EXHIBIT C - CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY

A conflict of interest exists when a director, officer, or key person has a financial, professional, personal, family, or organizational interest that could impair independent judgment in matters before the Corporation.

Any such person shall disclose the conflict promptly to the Board.

The Board shall determine whether a conflict exists.

A person with a conflict may provide background information if requested, but shall not be present for deliberation or vote on the matter and shall not attempt to improperly influence the decision.

The existence and resolution of any conflict shall be documented in the minutes, including the names of those present for discussion and vote.

Before initial election or appointment and annually thereafter, each director and officer shall submit a written disclosure statement identifying, to the best of their knowledge, any entities or relationships that could give rise to a conflict.

This policy shall be interpreted consistently with the New York Not-for-Profit Corporation Law and Internal Revenue Service requirements applicable to organizations described in Section 501(c)(3).

## EXHIBIT D - COMPENSATION AND REIMBURSEMENT POLICY

Directors shall not receive compensation for service as directors, but may receive reimbursement for reasonable, documented expenses incurred in service to the Corporation, if approved in accordance with this policy and applicable law.

The Corporation may pay reasonable compensation for services actually rendered in another capacity in furtherance of its charitable, educational, or scientific purposes.

Any compensation arrangement involving a director, officer, founder, or related party must be: (1) approved in advance by a majority of disinterested directors; (2) based on appropriate comparability data where reasonably available; (3) documented in the minutes together with the basis for the approval, contemporaneously with the decision; and (4) consistent with the Corporation's Conflict of Interest Policy and applicable law.

Reimbursements must be for reasonable and necessary expenses incurred in furtherance of the Corporation's purposes and must be supported by receipts or other documentation.

This policy shall be interpreted consistently with New York related-party transaction requirements and Internal Revenue Service rules concerning private inurement and excess benefit transactions.

## EXHIBIT E - PLATFORM GOVERNANCE MEMO

The SuperNova platform is a public-interest technology program of SuperNova 2177 Inc., developed as open-source software to advance the Corporation's charitable, educational, and scientific purposes.

The platform's governance shall be developed under Board oversight according to the following principles:

- Transparency - platform governance processes and policies should be publicly documented where appropriate.
- Attribution - AI-assisted activity should be clearly attributed and disclosed; AI participation in governance should be non-deceptive.
- Auditability - platform actions should be recorded in an audit trail that supports independent review and accountability.
- Human oversight - legally or materially consequential automated actions should require manual human review before execution.
- Consent-based participation - users should participate voluntarily with clear disclosure of how the platform operates.
- Record preservation - public-interest records should be preserved except where deletion is required by law or approved under lawful policy.
- Research and education - the Corporation may study, document, and educate the public about emerging questions concerning AI agency, digital rights, ethical human-AI collaboration, and constitutional principles in digital spaces.
The audit-trail architecture may document contributions in a manner capable of supporting future contribution-based governance models, provided that any such model remains consistent with the Corporation's nonprofit mission and applicable law.

The Board may revisit and refine these principles as the software, law, and research landscape evolve.

Adopted by the Board on: ______________________, 2026.
