# Bylaws

_Proposed bylaws for SuperNova 2177 Inc._

A New York Not-for-Profit Corporation

Adopted: ______________________, 2026

## ARTICLE I - NAME AND OFFICE

### Section 1.1. Name

The name of the corporation is SuperNova 2177 Inc. (the "Corporation").

### Section 1.2. Principal Office

The principal office of the Corporation shall be located in the State of New York, or at such other location as the Board of Directors may determine from time to time.

### Section 1.3. Registered Agent

The Corporation shall maintain a registered agent in the State of New York as required by law.

## ARTICLE II - PURPOSE

### Section 2.1. Charitable, Educational, and Scientific Purposes

The Corporation is organized and shall be operated exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

The Corporation's purposes include digital-rights education, public-interest software and open-source tools, research and education concerning ethical human-AI collaboration, arts and culture programs, fashion dignity and vocational support programs, and related charitable activities for underserved communities.

### Section 2.2. Limitations

No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, any director, officer, founder, or private person, except that the Corporation may pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and may reimburse reasonable expenses incurred in furtherance of the Corporation's purposes.

No substantial part of the Corporation's activities shall consist of carrying on propaganda or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, and the Corporation shall not participate or intervene in any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office.

### Section 2.3. Public-Interest Technology Programs

The Corporation may develop and maintain public-interest technology programs, including the SuperNova platform, under policies adopted by the Board from time to time.

Such policies may address transparency, attribution, auditability, consent-based participation, human oversight, digital-rights education, ethical AI collaboration research, and record-preservation practices consistent with applicable law and the Corporation's charitable, educational, and scientific purposes.

## ARTICLE III - BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 3.1. Powers

The affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by its Board of Directors (the "Board").

### Section 3.2. Number

The Board shall consist of not fewer than three (3) and not more than seven (7) directors.

The exact number of directors may be fixed from time to time by resolution of the Board within that range.

No decrease in the number of directors shall shorten the term of any incumbent director.

### Section 3.3. Qualifications

Directors shall be natural persons at least eighteen (18) years of age. Directors need not be residents of New York or the United States.

### Section 3.4. Terms

Directors shall serve two-year terms and until their successors are duly elected or appointed and qualified, unless they earlier resign, are removed, or otherwise cease to serve in accordance with law.

The Board may later adopt staggered terms by resolution or by amendment to these Bylaws.

### Section 3.5. Vacancies and Newly Created Directorships

Vacancies and newly created directorships may be filled by vote of a majority of the directors then in office, unless otherwise required by law, the Certificate of Incorporation, or these Bylaws.

A director appointed to fill a vacancy or newly created directorship shall serve for the term determined by the Board, subject to applicable law.

### Section 3.6. Resignation

A director may resign at any time by written notice to the President, Secretary, or the Board.

A resignation may be delivered by physical writing, email, electronic message, or other written electronic transmission if the identity and authorization of the resigning director can reasonably be determined.

A resignation is effective upon delivery unless it specifies a later effective date or a condition precedent, including appointment of one or more successor directors.

### Section 3.7. Removal

A director may be removed for cause by the Board at a meeting called for that purpose, provided that notice of the proposed removal has been given to all directors and the requirements of the New York Not-for-Profit Corporation Law are satisfied.

Removal without cause shall occur only if and to the extent permitted by applicable law.

## ARTICLE IV - MEETINGS

### Section 4.1. Annual Meeting

The Board shall hold at least one meeting each year, designated the Annual Meeting, to elect officers and conduct such other corporate business as may properly come before the Board.

### Section 4.2. Special Meetings

Special meetings may be called by the President or by any two directors.

Notice of a special meeting shall state the purpose of the meeting and shall be given at least forty-eight (48) hours in advance.

### Section 4.3. Notice and Waiver

Notice of meetings may be given by mail, email, telephone, text message, or any other reasonable means of communication.

Notice may be waived in writing before or after a meeting.

Attendance at a meeting constitutes waiver of notice unless the director attends for the express purpose of objecting to the transaction of business.

### Section 4.4. Quorum and Voting

A majority of the entire Board shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.

Each director shall have one vote.

Except as otherwise required by law, the Certificate of Incorporation, or these Bylaws, the vote of a majority of directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present shall be the act of the Board.

### Section 4.5. Remote Participation

Directors may participate in meetings by telephone, video conference, or similar means by which all participants can hear one another simultaneously and participate in all matters before the Board, including the ability to propose, object to, and vote on Board action.

Participation by such means constitutes presence in person.

### Section 4.6. Action Without a Meeting

Any action required or permitted to be taken by the Board may be taken without a meeting if all members of the Board consent to the adoption of a resolution authorizing the action.

Such consent may be written or electronic.

The resolution and written or electronic consents shall be filed with the minutes of the proceedings of the Board.

## ARTICLE V - OFFICERS

### Section 5.1. Officers

The officers of the Corporation shall be a President, a Secretary, and a Treasurer, and such other officers as the Board may elect.

Any two offices may be held by the same person, except the offices of President and Secretary.

### Section 5.2. Election and Term

Officers shall be elected by the Board and shall serve one-year terms and until their successors are elected and qualified, unless earlier removed or resigned.

### Section 5.3. Duties

The President shall be the chief executive officer of the Corporation, shall preside at Board meetings, and shall perform such other duties as the Board may assign.

The Secretary shall keep minutes of Board meetings, give required notices, maintain corporate records, and perform such other duties as the Board may assign.

The Treasurer shall have custody of corporate funds, maintain financial records, prepare financial reports for the Board, and perform such other duties as the Board may assign.

### Section 5.4. Removal and Resignation

Any officer may be removed by the Board with or without cause.

Any officer may resign by written notice to the Board.

## ARTICLE VI - COMMITTEES

### Section 6.1. Committees of the Board

The Board may establish committees of the Board consisting of three (3) or more directors, with authority only to the extent permitted by law and by Board resolution.

No committee shall have authority to fill vacancies, fix director compensation, amend the Bylaws, or take any other action reserved to the full Board by law.

### Section 6.2. Advisory Committees and Working Groups

The Board may create advisory committees or working groups that may include non-directors.

Advisory committees and working groups shall not exercise Board authority and shall serve solely in an advisory capacity.

## ARTICLE VII - CONFLICTS OF INTEREST AND COMPENSATION

### Section 7.1. Conflict of Interest Policy

The Board shall adopt and maintain a Conflict of Interest Policy consistent with the New York Not-for-Profit Corporation Law and Internal Revenue Service requirements.

Each director shall submit a written disclosure statement before initial election or appointment and annually thereafter.

### Section 7.2. Compensation and Reimbursement Policy

The Board shall adopt and maintain a Compensation and Reimbursement Policy.

Any compensation involving a director, officer, founder, or related party must be approved in advance by disinterested directors, based on appropriate comparability data where reasonably available, and documented in the minutes.

## ARTICLE VIII - FINANCES AND RECORDS

### Section 8.1. Fiscal Year

The fiscal year of the Corporation shall begin January 1 and end December 31.

### Section 8.2. Banking and Contracts

The Board shall authorize the opening of bank accounts and designate authorized signatories.

The Board may authorize officers or agents to enter into contracts on behalf of the Corporation.

### Section 8.3. Records and Filings

The Corporation shall maintain accurate books and records and shall make all filings required by federal and New York law.

## ARTICLE IX - INDEMNIFICATION

### Section 9.1. Indemnification

The Corporation shall indemnify its directors, officers, and authorized agents to the fullest extent permitted by the New York Not-for-Profit Corporation Law.

## ARTICLE X - AMENDMENTS

### Section 10.1. Amendments

These Bylaws may be amended, altered, or repealed by a two-thirds vote of the entire Board, provided the text of the proposed amendment has been provided to all directors at least ten (10) days before the meeting.

## ARTICLE XI - DISSOLUTION

### Section 11.1. Dissolution

Upon dissolution, the Corporation's assets shall be distributed for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, or to the federal government or to a state or local government for a public purpose, consistent with the Certificate of Incorporation.
